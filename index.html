The History of Phones

The Telegraph was the first electrical form of communication. In 1837 Samuel Morse developed his own electrical telegarph and pantented it in the U.S. His assistant, Alfred Vail, created the morse code, which is a language that transforms the noises from the telegraph to the alphabet. The noises were in form of a long beep and a short beep, or a dash and dot.

The Telephone was the first form of communication with your voices through electricity. Alexander Bell was the first person to invent the telephone. Bell was experimenting with his harmonic telegraph and he coul hear a sound coming from a wire. 10 months later he succefully spoke through a wire to his assistant saying "Mr. Watson, come here, I want to see you" The idea exploded over the years with telephone lines being invented.

Over the years the telephone system got better and different types of telephones were invented. There were Rotary dial which was better form of dialing the number you want to call instead of tap dialing. There were Candlestick Phones was sepreated into 2 peices, the mouth peice and the ear peice. These phones were popular in the 1890s and 1930s but got phased out when they just combined the two peices. The last major type was the cordless phone, so people can talk on the phone around without the stupid cord and still be able to call. These phones were super popular in the 1970's through the 2000's

The cellphone or the moblie phone is more advanced than those other phones. You are allowed to have the phone with you at all times and you are able to call anytime, anywhere you want if it is a telephone service area. There are so many of these areas now, so you will rarely accidentely be without service. The first handheld cellular mobile phone was demonstrated in 1973. These phones have spreaded everywhere, and when they got super small you can just fit it in your pocket. Some types of cellphones even allows you to text, email, and use the internet.
